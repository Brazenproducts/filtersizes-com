Why filter sizing trips people up

Most HVAC filters are sold by a nominal size, which is a rounded label like 16x20x1 or 20x25x4. The actual filter itself is usually a little smaller so it can slide into the cabinet or grille. That tiny difference is the reason a filter can look right on the box and still feel loose, jammed, or impossible to seat correctly.

If you understand three things, the process gets much easier: how to measure the opening, how nominal sizes differ from actual dimensions, and which common sizes show up most often in residential systems.

Fast replacement checklist

  • Pull out the old filter and read the printed size if it is still legible.
  • Measure width, height, and thickness with a tape measure if the label is faded or suspicious.
  • Check airflow direction before reinstalling.
  • Confirm whether your system uses a 1-inch filter or a deeper media cabinet.
  • Do not assume a filter that is close enough will seal correctly.
